## Biography
SHAOUL ABADA (The first son) ENGLISH: The roots of the Abada family begin in [[Places/Baghdad]], the capital of [[Places/Iraq]]. There was always an important Jewish community in this country, since the biblical exodus to Babel. In the end of the 19th century, all the Middle East was part of the Ottoman Empire. Then Yehezkel Abada, his wife and 7 children, (all boys), immigrate to [[Places/Egypt]], thru [[Places/Lebanon]] and [[Places/Palestine]]. The family lived in Tanta, a city in the Nile delta, north of [[Places/Cairo]]. Shaoul Abada, the first son, married with Regina Malka, and had 9 children. After the birth of Mary, the 7th child, the family moved to [[Places/Cairo]]. Shaoul, as his brothers, worked and dealed with textile. He died from "Phlebitis", in February 1951, at the Jewish hospital in [[Places/Cairo]]. --------------------------------- FRENCH: Les racines de la famille Abada, remontent a [[Places/Baghdad]], capitale d'[[Places/Iraq]], Depuis toujours, il y avait une importante communite juive, et cela depuis l'exode biblique de Babel En cette fin du 19 eme siecle , tout le moyen orient faisait partie de l'empire Ottoman.C'est alors que Yehezkel Abada, sa femme et ses 7 enfants, tous des garcons, emigrerent vers l'[[Places/Egypt]], via le Liban et la [[Places/Palestine]]. Ils s'etablirent a Tanta, ville dans le delta Egyptien au nord du [[Places/Cairo]]. Shaoul Abada, l'aine, se maria avec Regina Malka, et eurent 9 enfants. Apres la naissance de Mary, la 7 eme enfant, la famille demenagea au [[Places/Cairo]], ou Shaoul, comme la plupart de ses freres, travaillait comme commercant de textiles,... Il mourut de "Phlebite",en Fevrier 1951, a l'hopital Israelite du [[Places/Cairo]]. -----------------------------
