## Biography
ENGLISH: The roots of the Abada family begin in [[Places/Baghdad]], the capital of [[Places/Iraq]]. There was always an important Jewish community in this country, since the biblical exodus to Babel. In the end of the 19th century, all the Middle East was part of the Ottoman Empire. Then Yehezkel Abada, his wife and 7 children, (all boys), immigrate to [[Places/Egypt]], thru [[Places/Lebanon]] and [[Places/Palestine]]. The family lived in Tanta, a city in the Nile delta, north of [[Places/Cairo]]. In 1917, Moussa Abada, the third son, married with Freha Mekayess, and had 9 children. Later on the family moved to [[Places/Cairo]]. Moussa, like most of his brothers, worked with textile, and had a small shop in "Haret el Yahoud", that's the antique Jewish quarter in [[Places/Cairo]]. He died in 1946. Ils s'etablirent a Tanta, ville dans le delta Egyptien au nord du [[Places/Cairo]]. En 1917, Moussa Abada , 3eme garcon, se maria avec Freha Mekayess, et eurent 9 enfants. Plus tard la famille demenagea au [[Places/Cairo]]. Moussa, comme la plupart de ses freres, travaillait dans le textile et avait un petit magasin a "Haret El Yahoud", c'est l'antique quartier juif du [[Places/Cairo]].,...
