Town in present-day western Ukraine (Zakarpattia Oblast). Known historically as Tjacev (Czech), Tecső (Hungarian), and Tacovo. The Farkas and Aharon families originated here.

The town passed from Austro-Hungarian to Czechoslovak rule in 1920, then was shifted back to [[Places/Hungary]] before World War II. Its Jewish community of ~1,500 was devastated during the Holocaust — Jews were gathered in a central ghetto and deported to [[Places/Auschwitz]] and other camps between 1942–1944.
